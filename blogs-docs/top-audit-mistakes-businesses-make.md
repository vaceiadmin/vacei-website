# Top Audit Mistakes Businesses Make (and How to Avoid Them)

Every year, businesses go through audits — and many run into the same problems over and over again. Some mistakes are small, but others can cause serious delays, higher costs, or even penalties.

In this guide, we’ll highlight the **most common audit mistakes SMEs make**, explain why they happen, and show you how to avoid them.

---

![audit-mistakes-to-avoid.webp](/images/blog/audit-mistakes-to-avoid.webp)

## Mistake 1: Waiting Until the Last Minute

Too many companies start preparing only a few weeks before their audit deadline. By then, it’s often too late to fix missing records or answer questions properly.

**How to avoid it:**

- Start preparing right after your financial year-end.
- Keep your accounts and documents updated throughout the year.
- Book your auditor early so you’re not scrambling.

---

## Mistake 2: Poor Record-Keeping

Auditors need proof to support your financial statements. If invoices, receipts, or contracts are missing, they can’t verify your numbers.

**How to avoid it:**

- Store all invoices, bank statements, and contracts in one system.
- Use cloud storage or accounting software for easy access.
- Make sure records are labeled and organized.

---

## Mistake 3: Ignoring Related Party Transactions

Transactions with directors, shareholders, or related companies often raise red flags. If they’re not properly documented, auditors will question them.

**How to avoid it:**

- Document all loans, payments, or agreements with related parties.
- Disclose them clearly in your accounts.
- Review unusual transactions with your accountant before the audit.

---

## Mistake 4: Misunderstanding Deadlines

Different countries set strict deadlines for filing audited accounts. In **Malta**, for example, audited financial statements must be filed with the **Malta Business Registry (MBR)** within **10 months + 42 days** of year-end. Missing this leads to penalties.

**How to avoid it:**

- Know your country’s audit deadlines.
- Set reminders months in advance.
- Don’t leave auditor selection until the last moment.

---

## Mistake 5: Choosing the Wrong Auditor

Not all auditors have the same expertise. Picking someone without industry knowledge or overpaying for the wrong service can hurt your business.

**How to avoid it:**

- Compare multiple auditors before deciding.
- Check their qualifications and experience.
- Look for auditors who understand SMEs and startups, not just large corporations.

---

## Mistake 6: Assuming Your Accountant Will Handle Everything

Your accountant may prepare the books, but the audit is an independent process. Directors still need to be actively involved.

**How to avoid it:**

- Be ready to answer auditor questions.
- Review unusual items before the audit begins.
- Take responsibility for your financial statements.
