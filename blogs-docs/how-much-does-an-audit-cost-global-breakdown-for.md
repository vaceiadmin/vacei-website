# How Much Does an Audit Cost? Global Breakdown for SMEs

If you’re a business owner, one of the first questions you’ll ask when planning an audit is: **how much will it cost?** The answer depends on your company’s size, complexity, and location.

In this guide, we’ll break down the factors that affect audit pricing, compare costs in different countries, and explain how marketplaces like **VACEI** make it easier to find the right price for your business.

## Factors That Affect Audit Costs

The price of an audit isn’t fixed — it varies depending on several key factors:

- 📊 **Company size** – Larger companies with higher turnover and more employees usually pay more.
- 📚 **Complexity of accounts** – If your business has multiple subsidiaries, international operations, or unusual transactions, costs go up.
- 🕒 **Time required** – More hours = higher fees.
- 🌍 **Jurisdiction** – Costs differ significantly between countries.
- 📅 **Timing** – Rushed or last-minute audits may attract premium fees.

---

## Average Audit Costs Globally

Here’s a general breakdown of what SMEs can expect to pay for a statutory audit:

| **Country** | **Small Company Audit (SME)** | **Medium Company Audit** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Malta** | €2,000 – €5,000 | €5,000 – €15,000 |
| **UK** | £5,000 – £15,000 | £15,000 – £50,000 |
| **Ireland** | €5,000 – €12,000 | €12,000 – €40,000 |
| **Cyprus** | €2,500 – €7,000 | €7,000 – €20,000 |
| **USA** | $10,000 – $50,000 | $50,000+ |

👉 These are averages — actual prices vary depending on your auditor, sector, and deadlines.

---

## Why Costs Vary So Much

Some of the reasons audits can be more expensive in certain countries include:

- Higher regulatory requirements.
- Auditor licensing costs.
- More complex accounting standards (e.g., IFRS vs GAAP).
- Higher labor costs for professional services.

---

## Can SMEs Reduce Audit Costs?

Yes — while you can’t skip a statutory audit if the law requires it, you *can* reduce costs by:

- Keeping your financial records organized.
- Preparing supporting documents early.
- Avoiding last-minute audit requests.
- Comparing quotes from multiple auditors instead of sticking to one option.
