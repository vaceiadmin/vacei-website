## What Is an Audit and Why Does It Matter?

An audit is an independent review of your company’s financial statements, usually carried out by an external auditor.

Why it matters:

- 📌 **Legal compliance** : some businesses are required by law to have an audit.
- 📌 **Credibility** : audited accounts give banks, investors, and regulators confidence.
- 📌 **Transparency** : audits highlight risks and strengthen trust with stakeholders.

---

## Do You Legally Need an Audit?

Not every business is legally obliged to undergo an audit. Whether you need one depends on:

- **Local laws & thresholds** — for example, in Malta, GAPSME requires an audit if your company exceeds certain turnover, asset, or employee limits.
- **Investor requirements** — venture capital firms, shareholders, or lenders may require an audit even if the law doesn’t.
- **Industry regulations** — certain industries (finance, insurance, etc.) mandate audits for all companies.

💡 **Tip:** Even if your business falls below the legal threshold, an audit can still be beneficial when you’re planning for growth, applying for loans, or attracting investors.

## Signs Your Business Is Audit-Ready

Wondering if your business could pass an audit smoothly? Look for these signs:

- ✅ Your financial records are up to date and organized.
- ✅ Supporting documents (invoices, contracts, payroll) are easily accessible.
- ✅ You have basic internal controls (approvals, segregation of duties).
- ✅ No major unexplained or undocumented transactions exist.
- ✅ Directors and management are aligned and transparent.

If you tick most of these boxes, you’re already in a strong position.

---

## Common Mistakes That Delay Audits

Even businesses that legally need an audit often run into avoidable delays. The most common pitfalls are:

- Missing or incomplete documentation.
- Poor communication between directors and accountants.
- Last-minute scrambling to close the books.
- No agreed timeline for delivering auditor requests.

Avoiding these mistakes can cut weeks off your audit process.
