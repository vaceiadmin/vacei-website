# Do Startups in Malta Need an Audit? Rules for Small & Medium Businesses

If you’re launching a startup or running a small business in Malta, one of the first questions you’ll face is: **do we need an audit?**

The answer is often surprising. Unlike some countries that allow small companies to skip audits, Malta’s **Companies Act** requires *all* limited liability companies to have their accounts audited annually — no matter the size.

In this blog, we’ll break down what this means for startups and SMEs, how GAPSME rules apply, and why audits can actually help your business grow.

---

## Audit Rules for Startups in Malta

- Every company registered as a **limited liability company (Ltd.)** in Malta must prepare annual financial statements.
- These financial statements must be audited by a **licensed auditor in Malta**.
- Accounts and the auditor’s report must be filed with the **Malta Business Registry (MBR)** within the legal deadlines.

👉 **Key point:** Even if your business has zero revenue or minimal activity, you still need to file audited accounts.

---

## GAPSME and Small Business Reporting

Malta applies the **General Accounting Principles for Small and Medium-Sized Entities (GAPSME)** to make reporting easier for SMEs.

- **Small companies** (≤ €10m turnover, ≤ €5m assets, ≤ 50 employees) can use simplified financial statement formats.
- **Medium companies** (≤ €40m turnover, ≤ €20m assets, ≤ 250 employees) also benefit from reduced disclosures.
- **Large companies** must apply full IFRS reporting.

📌 While GAPSME reduces complexity, it doesn’t remove the audit requirement itself.

---

## Why Audits Are Useful for Startups

Many founders see audits only as a cost. But an audit can actually help your business in several ways:

- 📊 **Investor readiness** – investors prefer startups with audited accounts.
- 🏦 **Bank financing** – banks trust audited accounts when granting loans.
- 🧾 **Tax compliance** – ensures financial records are accurate and transparent.
- 🚀 **Growth planning** – an external review highlights risks and opportunities.

## Common Startup Mistakes with Audits

Startups in Malta often run into the same issues:

- ❌ Waiting until the deadline is near before finding an auditor.
- ❌ Thinking small size = no audit needed.
- ❌ Not keeping proper records from the start.
- ❌ Ignoring the MBR deadlines.

Fixing these mistakes early makes audits smoother and cheaper.
