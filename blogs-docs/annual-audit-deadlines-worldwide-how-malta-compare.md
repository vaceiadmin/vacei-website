# Annual Audit Deadlines Worldwide: How Malta Compares

Audit deadlines vary around the world, and missing them can lead to serious penalties. For directors and business owners, knowing when audited accounts are due is critical.

In Malta, the deadline is **10 months + 42 days** after year-end — but how does that compare to other countries? In this guide, we’ll look at **audit deadlines globally** and explain what Maltese businesses need to know.

## Why Audit Deadlines Matter

- 📌 **Legal compliance** – filing late can mean fines and reputational risk.
- 📌 **Investor trust** – timely audits signal good governance.
- 📌 **Banking and lending** – banks often require audited accounts on time to approve credit.

Audits aren’t just about compliance — they affect credibility and growth.

---

## Malta’s Audit Deadlines

Under the **Companies Act**:

- All limited liability companies must file audited accounts with the **Malta Business Registry (MBR)**.
- The deadline is **10 months + 42 days after the financial year-end**.

👉 Example: If your year-end is 31 December 2026, your deadline is **12 November 2025**.

Late filing = automatic MBR fines starting from €50 and increasing with delays.

👉 **Key takeaway:** Malta offers slightly more time than the UK and Ireland, but unlike those countries, *all* Maltese companies must file audited accounts.

## Common Mistakes with Deadlines

- ❌ Waiting until the last month to book an auditor.
- ❌ Misunderstanding the filing deadline vs audit completion.
- ❌ Missing MBR penalties and letting costs pile up.

💡 Pro tip: Always start audit prep within 3–4 months after year-end.
