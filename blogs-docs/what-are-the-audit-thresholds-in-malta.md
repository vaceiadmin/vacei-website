# What Are the Audit Thresholds in Malta? (Turnover, Assets, Employees Explained)

If you’re running a business in Malta, you’ve probably heard about *audit thresholds* under GAPSME. But what do these thresholds actually mean, and how do they affect your company?

The short answer: **all limited liability companies in Malta must have an audit**. Audit thresholds don’t decide *whether* you need an audit — they decide *how detailed your reporting needs to be*. In this blog, we’ll break down Malta’s thresholds, show you where your company fits, and compare them with other countries.

---

## Why Audit Thresholds Exist

Not all companies are the same. A small family-run business doesn’t need the same reporting detail as a large corporation. To avoid overburdening smaller firms, Malta applies different rules under the **General Accounting Principles for Small and Medium-Sized Entities (GAPSME)**.

👉 **Key point:** Thresholds simplify *reporting requirements*, not *audit obligations*. Every company still needs an audit.

---

## GAPSME Audit Thresholds in Malta

Under GAPSME, a company is classified as *small*, *medium*, or *large*. The thresholds are based on three criteria: **turnover, balance sheet total, and number of employees**.

### **Small Companies** (any two of the following):

- Turnover ≤ €10 million
- Balance sheet total ≤ €5 million
- Employees ≤ 50

### **Medium Companies** (any two of the following):

- Turnover ≤ €40 million
- Balance sheet total ≤ €20 million
- Employees ≤ 250

### **Large Companies**

- Exceeding the above limits.

📌 Small and medium-sized entities can use simplified GAPSME disclosures. Large companies must follow full IFRS reporting standards.

---

## How This Affects Your Business

- If your company is **small**: you still need an audit, but your notes and disclosures in the financial statements are simplified.
- If your company is **medium**: more disclosures are required, but you’re still under GAPSME.
- If your company is **large**: you must apply **International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS)**, which are more detailed and complex.

## Malta vs Other Countries: Audit Thresholds Compared

Here’s how Malta’s thresholds compare with a few EU countries:

| **Country** | **Small Company Thresholds** | **Audit Requirement** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Malta** | ≤ €10m turnover, ≤ €5m assets, ≤ 50 employees | Audit mandatory for *all* companies |
| **UK** | ≤ £10.2m turnover, ≤ £5.1m assets, ≤ 50 employees | Audit exemption possible if under all thresholds |
| **Ireland** | ≤ €12m turnover, ≤ €6m assets, ≤ 50 employees | Audit exemption possible |
| **Cyprus** | ≤ €10m turnover, ≤ €5m assets, ≤ 50 employees | Audit mandatory for *all* companies |

👉 **Key takeaway:** Unlike the UK or Ireland, **Malta does not allow audit exemptions**. Even if your company is small, you still need a statutory audit.

## Common Mistakes Companies Make with Thresholds

Many business owners misunderstand audit thresholds in Malta. Here are the usual errors:

- ❌ Believing small companies don’t need audits (they do).
- ❌ Confusing thresholds with exemption rules (in Malta, there are none).
- ❌ Not monitoring growth — crossing thresholds can change reporting obligations.
- ❌ Assuming IFRS is optional (for large companies, it’s mandatory).
