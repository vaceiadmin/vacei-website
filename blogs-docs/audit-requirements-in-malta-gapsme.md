# Audit Requirements in Malta: Do You Need One Under GAPSME?

For many business owners in Malta, the word *audit* raises more questions than answers. Do you actually need one? What does Maltese law say? And how do the rules here compare with other countries?

The truth is simple: under Malta's **Companies Act** and **GAPSME framework**, certain businesses *must* undergo an annual statutory audit. But not every company falls into that category. In this blog, we'll explain the requirements in Malta, the thresholds to look out for, and how these rules compare with international standards.

![Audit Requirements in Malta](/images/blog/audit-requirements-malta-gapsme.webp "Audit Requirements in Malta - GAPSME Framework")

## Why Audits Matter in Malta

An audit is more than just a legal box to tick. It provides:

- 📌 **Legal compliance** – meeting your obligations under the Companies Act.
- 📌 **Credibility** – audited accounts increase trust with banks, investors, and regulators.
- 📌 **Transparency** – audits highlight risks and strengthen confidence in your business.

In short, an audit protects your company's reputation while ensuring you're in line with the law.

---

## Audit Requirements Under Maltese Law

All **limited liability companies (Ltd.) registered in Malta** are subject to statutory audit requirements. The **Companies Act (Chapter 386 of the Laws of Malta)** makes it clear:

- Every company must prepare annual financial statements.
- These statements must be audited by a **Maltese Certified Public Accountant (CPA) holding a practicing certificate in auditing**.
- Financial statements and audit reports must be filed with the **Malta Business Registry (MBR)**.

There are **no exemptions** — even small private companies fall under these rules. However, the **level of detail in reporting** may differ depending on size, as determined by GAPSME thresholds.

---

## GAPSME: How Audit Thresholds Work

The **General Accounting Principles for Small and Medium-Sized Entities (GAPSME)** sets out thresholds that determine reporting requirements. While *all companies must be audited*, the size of your business affects the **type of disclosures** required.

Here are the main thresholds:

- **Small companies** (any two of the following):
    - Turnover ≤ €10 million
    - Balance sheet total ≤ €5 million
    - Employees ≤ 50
- **Medium companies** (any two of the following):
    - Turnover ≤ €40 million
    - Balance sheet total ≤ €20 million
    - Employees ≤ 250

If your company falls within these ranges, you may apply simplified GAPSME reporting rules — but the **audit itself is still mandatory**.

## Malta vs International Audit Rules

Here's a quick comparison showing how Malta's audit requirements stack up against other countries:

| **Country** | **Audit Requirement** | **Exemptions / Thresholds** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Malta** | All limited liability companies must be audited | None – all companies require audit (GAPSME adjusts reporting detail) |
| **UK** | Audit required unless SME exemption applies | Exempt if ≤ £10.2m turnover, ≤ £5.1m assets, ≤ 50 employees |
| **Ireland** | Similar to UK | Audit exemption if ≤ €12m turnover, ≤ €6m assets, ≤ 50 employees |
| **Cyprus** | Audit mandatory for all companies | No exemption for small entities |

👉 **Key takeaway:** Malta is stricter than many EU countries — even the smallest private limited company is required to have an annual audit.
