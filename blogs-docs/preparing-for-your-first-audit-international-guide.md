# Preparing for Your First Audit: International Guide + Malta Rules Explained

If you’ve never gone through an audit before, the process can seem overwhelming. What documents do you need? How long will it take? And what rules apply in your country?

The good news is: with the right preparation, your first audit doesn’t have to be stressful. In this guide, we’ll explain **how to prepare for your first audit**, what most businesses need worldwide, and the **specific rules for Malta**.

---

![first-audit-guide-malta-international.webp](/images/blog/first-audit-guide-malta-international.webp)

## Why Audits Matter

Audits are independent reviews of your company’s financial statements. They matter because they:

- ✔️ Ensure legal compliance with accounting laws.
- ✔️ Build trust with banks, investors, and regulators.
- ✔️ Provide transparency for directors and shareholders.

In many countries, audits are **legally required** once your company reaches certain thresholds.

---

## Global Audit Preparation: What Every Business Needs

No matter where you are, businesses preparing for an audit should:

1. **Close accounts** – finalize income, expenses, payroll, and tax filings.
2. **Organize supporting documents** – invoices, receipts, contracts, and bank statements.
3. **Review unusual transactions** – loans, related-party dealings, or one-off items.
4. **Choose the right auditor** – licensed, experienced, and aligned with your industry.
5. **Stay involved** – directors must sign off and communicate with auditors.

---

## Malta-Specific Audit Rules

In Malta, **all limited liability companies** must undergo an annual statutory audit — even small startups.

- 📌 **Companies Act requirement** – every Ltd. must have audited accounts filed with the **Malta Business Registry (MBR)**.
- 📌 **Deadlines** – audited accounts must be filed within **10 months + 42 days** of year-end.
- 📌 **GAPSME thresholds** – small and medium companies can use simplified reporting, but audits are still mandatory.
- 📌 **Large companies** – must follow full IFRS reporting.

👉 Unlike the UK or Ireland, Malta does not allow audit exemptions for small companies.

---

## Timeline: How Long Does an Audit Take?

- Small companies: **2–6 weeks** depending on how organized records are.
- Medium companies: **6–12 weeks**.
- Large companies: several months, depending on complexity.

💡 Tip: Start early. Don’t wait until the deadline is close — auditors need time to review and finalize.

---

## Common First-Time Mistakes

- ❌ Thinking small size = no audit (not true in Malta).
- ❌ Waiting until the last month to find an auditor.
- ❌ Poor documentation (missing invoices, contracts, or bank statements).
- ❌ Assuming the accountant will handle everything (directors must stay involved).
