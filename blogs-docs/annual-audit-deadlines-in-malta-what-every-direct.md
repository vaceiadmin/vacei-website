# Annual Audit Deadlines in Malta: What Every Director Should Know

Running a company in Malta comes with strict deadlines for filing audited financial statements. Missing them can lead to fines, penalties, and problems with the Malta Business Registry (MBR).

In this guide, we’ll explain the **audit filing deadlines in Malta**, what directors need to prepare, and how to avoid last-minute stress.

---

![malta-audit-deadlines-mbr.webp](/images/blog/malta-audit-deadlines-mbr.webp)

## Why Deadlines Matter

In Malta, **all limited liability companies** are required by the **Companies Act** to prepare audited financial statements every year. These must then be filed with the **MBR**.

If you miss the deadline:

- 📌 You face **administrative penalties** from the MBR.
- 📌 Late filings can affect your company’s **reputation** with banks and investors.
- 📌 Repeat delays may trigger further compliance investigations.

---

## Malta’s Audit Deadlines Explained

Here’s how the system works:

- A company’s **financial year** usually runs for **12 months** (though the first may be longer or shorter).
- **Audited financial statements** must be **filed with the MBR within 10 months plus 42 days** of the financial year end.
- Example: If your year-end is **31 December 2026**, your deadline to file is **12 November 2025**.

📌 The MBR calculates penalties automatically if you miss the deadline — the later you are, the higher the fine.

---

## Audit Timeline for Directors

To avoid penalties, directors should plan ahead. A typical audit timeline looks like this:

1. **Month 1–2 after year-end:** Close accounts with your accountant.
2. **Month 3–6:** Provide documents to auditors (invoices, contracts, payroll, bank statements).
3. **Month 7–9:** Auditors review, discuss adjustments, and prepare their report.
4. **By Month 10:** Directors approve audited accounts and submit to MBR.

👉 Leaving it until the last 2 months is risky — auditors may not have enough time if there are delays.

---

## Penalties for Late Filing in Malta

The MBR applies fines if you miss your filing deadline. Penalties depend on how late you are, starting from **€50–€600 or more** depending on the length of the delay and company size.

Besides fines, **persistent late filing** can also lead to:

- Legal enforcement actions.
- Difficulty opening or renewing bank accounts.
- Negative signals to investors or regulators.
