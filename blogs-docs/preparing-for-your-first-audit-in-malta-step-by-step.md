# Preparing for Your First Audit in Malta: Step-by-Step Guide

Getting ready for your first audit can feel overwhelming — especially if you’ve never been through the process before. The good news is that with the right preparation, an audit doesn’t have to be stressful.

In Malta, all limited liability companies are required to have an annual audit under the **Companies Act**. Whether you’re a startup or an established SME, this guide will walk you through the steps you need to take to prepare.

---

![first-audit-preparation-malta.webp](/images/blog/first-audit-preparation-malta.webp)

## Step 1: Close Your Accounts

The first step is to finalize your financial records for the year. This means:

- Making sure all income and expenses are recorded.
- Reconciling your bank accounts.
- Ensuring payroll, tax, and VAT filings are up to date.

📌 Directors are responsible for ensuring the accounts give a true and fair view of the company’s position.

---

## Step 2: Gather Supporting Documents

Auditors will ask for proof to back up your numbers. Be ready with:

- Invoices and receipts.
- Contracts and agreements.
- Bank statements.
- Payroll records.
- Loan agreements.

👉 Keeping these organized will save you time and reduce back-and-forth with your auditor.

---

## Step 3: Review Unusual Transactions

Auditors will pay special attention to transactions that are:

- Unusual in size or nature.
- Related to directors, shareholders, or group companies.
- Without clear documentation.

Tip: Discuss these with your accountant before the audit so you can explain them properly.

---

## Step 4: Understand Deadlines

In Malta, audited financial statements must be filed with the **Malta Business Registry (MBR)** within **10 months + 42 days** of the financial year end.

Example: If your financial year ends on **31 December 2026**, you must file by **12 November 2025**.

Missing the deadline = fines and penalties.

---

## Step 5: Choose the Right Auditor

Not all auditors have the same experience. When selecting one:

- Check if they’re licensed in Malta.
- Ask about experience with your industry.
- Compare fees and timelines.

---

## Step 6: Stay Involved During the Audit

An audit isn’t just about handing over documents. Be prepared to:

- Answer questions from your auditor.
- Approve any adjustments they recommend.
- Sign off on the final accounts.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

First-time audits often go wrong because businesses:

- ❌ Wait until the last month before looking for an auditor.
- ❌ Don’t keep documents organized throughout the year.
- ❌ Assume their accountant will handle everything without their input.
